Frequently Asked Questions About Digital Marketing for Dermatologists

Answers dermatologists ask most about getting more patients and growing with AI.

How do dermatology practices get more patients online?

The most effective approach combines Google Ads targeting both medical and cosmetic dermatology searches, local SEO for map pack visibility, a website showcasing your treatments and credentials, and an AI Voice Agent that answers every call. Dermatology practices lose patients because phone lines are busy during peak hours and closed after 5pm when patients are researching cosmetic procedures. The AI captures those leads instantly.

What is the best way to market a dermatology practice?

Google Ads targeting "dermatologist near me," "skin check," "Botox cost," and "acne treatment [city]" reach patients ready to book. Separate campaigns for medical and cosmetic services allow precise targeting. Combine with landing pages showcasing before/after results, Google Business Profile optimization, local SEO, and an AI that books every inquiry.

How much do Google Ads cost for dermatologists?

Cost per click ranges from $8 to $40 depending on procedure type — cosmetic keywords like Botox and fillers tend to run higher due to competition, but the procedure values justify the spend. A $30 click that becomes a recurring cosmetic patient worth $2,000+ annually is excellent ROI. Salois Digital tracks cost per new patient across both medical and cosmetic channels.

Why do dermatology practices miss so many patient calls?

Dermatology front desks handle complex scheduling — different appointment types, multiple providers, referral processing, and insurance verification. When calls stack up, patients get sent to voicemail. Cosmetic patients often research in the evening when the office is closed. The AI Voice Agent handles both scenarios, answering instantly 24/7.

How does the AI Voice Agent work for dermatology practices?

When a patient calls and lines are busy, the AI answers within two seconds. It determines whether the patient needs medical or cosmetic care, captures their concern, insurance details, and scheduling preferences, and books the appropriate appointment type. It can answer questions about your services, accepted insurances, and consultation processes.

Is SEO worth it for dermatology practices?

Absolutely. Patients research dermatologists extensively before booking — especially for cosmetic procedures. Ranking for "best dermatologist in [city]" and specific treatment searches generates free, high-intent leads month after month. We optimize for both medical and cosmetic search terms to capture the full range of patient intent.

How fast can a dermatology practice start getting more patients?

Google Ads generate patient inquiries within the first week. Most practices see new appointments within two weeks. SEO builds over 60 to 90 days. The combination of ads and AI call handling means you're booking patients immediately.

What makes Salois Digital different for dermatology practices?

We build separate campaigns for medical and cosmetic dermatology, understanding that these patient types search differently and convert differently. The AI Voice Agent handles both — booking skin checks during the day and cosmetic consultations from evening inquiries. We track new patients by service line so you know exactly what's working.
