Frequently Asked Questions About Small Business Website Development

What local business owners need to know about getting a website that actually generates leads and booked appointments.

Why does my small business need a new website?

Your website is the first impression most customers will have of your business. When someone searches for your service on Google, clicks on your ad, or finds you through a referral, they visit your website before deciding whether to call. If your site loads slowly, looks outdated on mobile, buries your phone number, or doesn’t clearly explain what you do and why they should choose you — they leave and call your competitor instead. A conversion-optimized website doesn’t just look professional; it’s engineered to guide every visitor toward a specific action: calling your business, filling out a form, or booking an appointment. If your current website isn’t generating consistent leads, it’s costing you money every single day.

How is a Salois Digital website different from using a website builder like Wix or Squarespace?

DIY website builders make it easy to create a website, but “easy to create” and “designed to convert visitors into customers” are two very different things. Wix and Squarespace templates are generic — they weren’t built for your industry, your local market, or your specific conversion goals. They often load slowly due to bloated code, lack proper SEO structure, and make it difficult to implement advanced tracking or integrate with tools like AI call handling. We build every site from scratch with one objective: turning website visitors into booked appointments. Every page layout, button placement, headline, and call to action is strategically designed based on what actually drives conversions for local service businesses — not what looks nice in a template gallery.

How long does it take to build my website?

Most websites are fully live within 14 days of kickoff. During the first few days, we audit your current online presence, gather your business details, and define the site structure and conversion goals. Design and development takes roughly a week, followed by your review period where we make any revisions. Once approved, we launch with analytics, call tracking, and AI Voice Agent integration already configured. Compare this to the 6–12 weeks (or longer) that traditional web agencies typically take, and you start generating leads months sooner. We move fast because we know every day without a high-converting website is a day you’re losing potential customers.

Will my website be mobile-friendly?

Absolutely — and mobile-friendly is actually the baseline, not the goal. We design mobile-first, meaning we start with the mobile experience and then scale up to tablet and desktop. This matters because over 60% of local service searches happen on smartphones, and Google uses mobile-first indexing — meaning Google primarily looks at the mobile version of your site when deciding where to rank you. Every element is optimized for touch interactions, tap-to-call buttons are prominently placed, and text is readable without zooming. Your site will load fast and look great on every device, from the latest iPhone to a three-year-old Android.

How does the website integrate with your AI Voice Agent?

The integration is seamless. Every click-to-call button on your website routes directly to your AI Voice Agent, which means calls from your site are answered instantly — 24 hours a day, 7 days a week — even if you’re closed, on another call, or out on a job. The AI captures the caller’s information, qualifies the lead, and books appointments into your calendar. We also add smart contact forms that capture lead details for visitors who prefer not to call. The result is a closed loop: your website drives traffic, the AI captures every lead, and you never lose a potential customer because nobody picked up the phone. It’s the missing link between getting traffic and actually booking revenue.

Will my website be optimized for Google and search engines?

Every website we build is SEO-ready from day one. This includes clean semantic HTML that search engines can easily crawl, optimized title tags and meta descriptions for every page, proper heading hierarchy, image optimization with alt text, structured data (schema markup) for local business and service information, and fast Core Web Vitals scores that Google rewards in rankings. We also build your site architecture to support long-term SEO growth — with dedicated service pages, location pages if applicable, and a structure that makes it easy to add content over time. If you combine the website with our organic search marketing service, we handle ongoing SEO so your site climbs the rankings month after month.

Do I own my website? Can I take it with me if I leave?

Yes — you own your website, your domain, and all of your content. We don’t hold your business hostage with proprietary platforms or lock-in contracts. If you ever decide to move on, we’ll hand over all files, assets, and access. This is a major differentiator from agencies that build on closed platforms where you can’t take your site with you. We believe in earning your business through results, not trapping you with technology. Your website is your property, period.

What does website development cost for a small business?

Pricing depends on the scope of your project — a 5-page service site for a plumber has different needs than a 20-page multi-location medical practice site. That said, our packages are designed specifically for small business budgets and include everything: design, development, mobile optimization, SEO setup, analytics integration, AI Voice Agent connection, and launch. There are no hidden fees for hosting, SSL, or basic maintenance. Visit our pricing page for package details, or contact us for a custom quote based on your business.

What happens after my website launches?

Launch is just the beginning. Once your site is live, we monitor its performance — tracking page load speed, visitor behavior, call volume, form submissions, and conversion rates. We use this data to make ongoing improvements: tweaking headlines, adjusting call-to-action placement, updating content, and optimizing pages that underperform. If you’re also running Google Ads with us, we continuously align your landing pages with your ad campaigns for maximum ROI. Your website isn’t a set-it-and-forget-it project — it’s a living growth tool that gets better over time as we learn what works best for your specific audience.
